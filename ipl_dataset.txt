IPL Impact Players and Rules
The Impact Player rule was introduced in IPL 2023. Teams can substitute one player during the match as an impact player. This rule changed team strategies significantly. Bowlers could now be replaced by extra batsmen. The powerplay has six overs where only two fielders are allowed outside the circle. DRS or Decision Review System is used in IPL matches. The super over is used to decide tied matches. Pink ball is used for all IPL matches as they are played under lights. Teams wear different colored jerseys to distinguish them. Third umpire reviews close calls during IPL matches. Helmet cameras and stump cameras give unique views during IPL. Hot spot and snickometer technologies help in reviewing decisions. Dew plays a crucial role in evening matches affecting the toss decision. The team winning the toss usually chooses to field first due to dew. Captains make crucial decisions about batting or bowling first.

IPL Batting Techniques
The art of batting in IPL requires a different mindset from Test cricket. Batsmen need to score quickly from the first ball they face. Opening batsmen set the tone with aggressive stroke play in powerplay overs. The powerplay consists of the first six overs where only two fielders are outside the circle. Teams try to score at least fifty runs in the powerplay to set a good base. Middle-order batsmen need to accelerate when wickets fall in the middle overs. The death overs from over sixteen to twenty require specialized hitting skills. Batsmen practice hitting yorkers and bouncers over the boundary for six. Switch hits and ramp shots have become common in modern IPL batting. The Dilscoop invented by Tillakaratne Dilshan helped batsmen score over the keeper. Reverse sweeps have become a common weapon against fast bowlers bowling full tosses. Batting with a deep grip helps generate maximum power for big hitting. Left-handed batsmen like Warner and Gayle are particularly effective in IPL. Opening pairs that complement each other with left-right combination are very effective. Batsmen who can accelerate from ball one are most valued in IPL auctions.

IPL Bowling Strategies
Bowling in IPL requires immense skill and tactical awareness. Fast bowlers use variations like slower balls, cutters and yorkers to deceive batsmen. The slower ball cutter is one of the most effective weapons in death overs. Swing bowling with the new ball in powerplay is extremely valuable for teams. Night cricket in IPL creates a unique and electrifying atmosphere for spectators. Stadium lights illuminate the ground creating a theatre-like experience for fans. The transition period between twilight and full darkness can be challenging for batsmen. Stadiums have invested in better lighting technology to reduce shadows on the field.
